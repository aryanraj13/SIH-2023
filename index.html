Student dropout in India is an alarming concern. With substantial numbers of students dropping out of schools and educational platforms prematurely, this issue not only hampers individual prospects but also poses a significant obstacle to the nation's overall growth. It perpetuates socio-economic disparities, curtails economic growth, and underscores the urgent need for a comprehensive assessment of the education system's effectiveness and inclusivity.

Learn More

In India, student dropout rates can be attributed to a complex interplay of factors. Economic hardships, limited access to quality education, financial constraints, familial obligations, and subpar educational infrastructure all contribute to this issue. Moreover, societal pressures, early marriages, and child labor practices further exacerbate the problem. To combat these challenges effectively, a comprehensive approach is needed, focusing on equitable access to education and robust support systems for students.

In our study of student dropout rates in India, a robust dropout analysis method is vital. It untangles complex factors and enables targeted interventions, proving essential in addressing this issue.
